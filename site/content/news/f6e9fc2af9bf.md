Today, nuclear energy enjoys rare support across the political spectrum.

Public approval has spiked, and Big Tech is throwing money around to meet rising electricity demand.

That newfound interest is exactly why it’s time to talk about an old problem: nuclear waste.
In the US, nuclear reactors produce about 2,000 metric tons of high-level waste each year—and there’s nowhere to put it.

Now, the need for a permanent storage solution is becoming urgent.
